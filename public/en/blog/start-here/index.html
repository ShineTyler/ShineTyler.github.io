The early version of a personal site is often more useful than people expect. You may not have internships, publications, or income yet. That is fine. A good site can still show taste, consistency, and initiative.

What to publish first

  • A short introduction that explains what you study and what you are trying to get better at.
  • Two or three projects with screenshots, links, and one paragraph of context.
  • One honest blog post about what you are learning right now.

What to avoid

  • Copying the tone of a company website when your real advantage is curiosity and sincerity.
  • Listing too many technologies without showing what you built with them.
  • Waiting for everything to be perfect before you publish anything.

A simple rhythm

Try a small publishing rhythm such as one note each week or one project writeup every two weeks. The goal is not volume. The goal is to make your future self easier to trust.
